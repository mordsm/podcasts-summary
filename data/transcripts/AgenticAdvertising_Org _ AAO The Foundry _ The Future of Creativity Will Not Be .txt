Event: The Foundry by AgenticAdvertising.Org (AAO)

Title: The Future of Creativity Will Not Be Automated

Speakers: 

Colleen DeCourcy, CMO, Sonos

For forty years the economic structure of marketing moved creativity to the cheap side of the ledger. Media procurement generated the margins. The brief sat on the wrong side of the P&L. The result was a very fast, very efficient machine for distributing work that wasn't worth distributing.

The agentic era closes that arbitrage. When creative and media operate as one continuous system, the gap that margin was extracted from disappears. Average work becomes economically inefficient in real time. The advantage moves to the only thing that can't be optimized away.

But this isn't a creative revolution. It's a correction. A long overdue, structurally enforced, economically inevitable correction. Creativity itself hasn't changed. What's changing is what it's worth.

Colleen DeCourcy, CMO of Sonos and former Global CCO of Wieden+Kennedy, traces the arc from her 2018 IAB talk predicting marketing's infrastructure would become its own friction, through the reality of a brand turnaround, to what the agentic era actually requires of the people building it. Not better tooling. Better briefs. Every agent, protocol, and optimization loop runs from the first true thing, or it runs from nothing.

Your agent isn't an idea. It never will be. Welcome to being right at scale.
