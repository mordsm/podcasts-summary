Any athlete knows they need to train in advance to reach peak performance. Olympians train anywhere from 15–40 hours a week, depending on their sport. And even if you aren’t aiming for a gold medal, it’s recommended that you get at least 30 minutes of physical exercise a day.

Likewise, if we want our minds to be prepared when high-stress situations arise, we need to train them, too. In this video lesson, neuroscientist Amish Jha explains how you can cultivate your mental fitness in just 12 minutes a day — less time than it takes to exercise your body!
