[SHOW NOTES]
David Marcus says Oren Zeev has a superhuman ability to read people — and it shows in how he invests. Oren breaks down exactly what he looks for when a founder walks into Café Venetia: an unfair competitive advantage over other VCs, a real shot at market leadership, founders he believes in, and at least one signal that product-market fit is real — even if it's just 5 new paying customers a day with zero marketing.
Managing nearly $3 billion solo, Oren moves fast. Most decisions take under 48 hours, with one key question to validate. He explains how he vetted Navan (then TripActions) with just a few CFO phone calls — and why that was enough.
If you want to understand how the best solo VCs think, this is it.
