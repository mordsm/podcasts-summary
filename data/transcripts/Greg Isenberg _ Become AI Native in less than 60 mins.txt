Numbered Section Summaries

1. Defining AI Native: I press Theo for a concrete definition, and he lands on three pillars: people manage agents, agents read and write to the company, and the company grows smarter over time. This frames the whole episode and separates a true AI native org from a team that simply opens ChatGPT.

2. People Own The Bookends: Theo explains that AI now handles the middle execution while people focus on the high-value bookends: strategy, taste, judgment, and communication. The reframe is simple: everyone becomes a manager of agents, and success follows the output of that team.

3. Agents And Autonomy: We treat agents like new hires who earn autonomy once they have four things: a clear goal, the right skills, the right tools, and rich context. With all four in place, agents run for days and deliver work that holds up to a real quality bar, which evals make visible.

4. Skills And Skill Chains: Theo shares LCA's skills library and the idea of a skill chain: a macro skill that fires several skills in sequence. Chaining a build skill with a copy skill and a QA skill raises quality and keeps every claim tied to transcripts and data.

5. The Proposal Demo: Theo triggers a proposal skill chain for a Spotify prospect, and within minutes a branded proposal microsite appears, complete with plan, team, and cost. It weaves in personal moments pulled from past calls, like Maya's record-store metaphor and my November marathon, which is exactly how speed turns into trust and closed deals.

6. The Context Layer: Theo reveals the brain: folders of markdown files that agents search, retrieve from, and write back to. A loop of capture, curate, store, execute, and experience keeps the company readable to agents and feeds market signal right back into the system.

7. Live Prototype And Signal: Theo builds a Daily Blitz feature by voice in under ten minutes, ships it to a labs page, and runs a usability test on the spot. The signal tab synthesizes responses into lessons and stands ready to plan and execute a V2 in the same session.

8. Productizing The System: We turn the playbook into startup ideas: package this people-plus-agents-plus-context system as a service and niche down by industry, function, and company size.
