[SHOW NOTES]
Every VC right now says the same thing: "2x growth isn't good enough. We only invest in 10x companies." And Oren Zeev thinks it's one of the most dangerous ideas in venture.
Oren — who manages $3 billion solo and has backed Navan, Tipalti, Audible, and more — breaks down why the obsession with hypergrowth isn't just wrong, it's actively harmful. A company growing 2x sustainably at scale — from $20M to $40M to $80M to $160M — is 64x bigger in six years. The math doesn't change.
What does change is the pressure. When VCs demand unsustainable growth rates, founders find ways to manufacture it. In insurance? Easy: just misprice your policies and watch the numbers go up. The growth looks great until it doesn't — and then the whole thing collapses.
Oren's take: the "10x or nothing" narrative exists because it sounds impressive, not because it reflects reality. And it's created a generation of companies chasing metrics they can't sustain.
This is the kind of honest, contrarian thinking that makes Oren one of the most respected voices in venture.
