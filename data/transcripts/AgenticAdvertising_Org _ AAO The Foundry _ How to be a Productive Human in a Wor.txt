Title: Agents & Change Agents: How to be a Productive Human in a World of Intelligent Machines

Speakers: 

Tom Griffiths, Henry R. Luce Professor of Information Technology, Consciousness and Culture, Princeton University

The ability to use AI to generate text, images, and code is changing the way we work. What skills should we be building in response? Tom Griffiths argues that in a world of intelligent machines, the ability to evaluate work is more important than the ability to generate it, and metacognition (choosing what to think about) is more important than cognition. Using examples from recent studies of human and machine behavior to support these points, Griffiths will give attendees a guide to how to be a productive human in this agentic future.
